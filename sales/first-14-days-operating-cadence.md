# MCPScan First 14 Days Operating Cadence

Use this after domain, mailbox, and Stripe links are live. The goal is not brand awareness. The goal is paid audit conversations and first revenue.

## Daily Non-Negotiables

Every business day:

- source 10 new accounts
- qualify 6 accounts to score 6+
- prepare 6 personalized outbound drafts
- get exact-recipient and exact-message approval before sending
- send only approved messages
- follow up on due conversations
- update the pipeline before stopping

Weekly target:

- 50 new sourced accounts
- 30 qualified accounts
- 30 approved/sent messages
- 10 replies or conversations
- 3 serious sales calls
- 1 paid audit or strong checkout-intent conversation

## Stage Rules

| Stage | Move In When | Move Out When |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Research | account has an MCP/agent trigger | source URL, buyer persona, and trigger are captured |
| Qualified | score is 6+ or trigger is unusually strong | exact recipient and draft angle are ready |
| Approved to contact | exact recipient and exact message are approved in the same turn | message is sent manually through approved channel |
| Interested | prospect replies, asks for details, or accepts scope | package and checkout next step are approved |
| Paid | Stripe confirms payment | safe intake and private workspace are created |
| Delivered | report is sent | findings call/re-scan/testimonial path is handled |
| Closed lost | buyer declines or no response after final follow-up | reason is captured |

## Day-By-Day Plan

### Day 1

- Buy domain and mailbox if not done.
- Create Stripe Payment Links if not done.
- Apply live links to landing page.
- Source 20 accounts from GitHub, LinkedIn, vendor docs, MCP launch posts, and security discussions.
- Prepare first 6 outreach drafts for approval.

### Day 2

- Send approved Day 1 messages.
- Source 10 more accounts.
- Prepare 6 more drafts.
- Follow any replies with the battlecard qualification questions.

### Day 3

- Send approved follow-ups or new messages.
- Ask interested prospects whether the use case is internal rollout, customer pilot, or enterprise review.
- Move strong replies to `Interested`.
- Offer the one-page scope or Launch Audit checkout path.

### Day 4

- Source 10 agency or consultant prospects.
- Send agency-focused messages only after approval.
- Use Quick Audit or Launch Audit depending on client workflow complexity.

### Day 5

- Review pipeline by stage.
- Close weak accounts with no clear trigger.
- Prepare the next 15 accounts for the following week.
- Publish or prep one community post only if it does not distract from direct outreach.

### Day 6

- Follow up on Day 1 sends.
- Offer sample report to warm prospects.
- Ask direct qualification questions before sending checkout links.

### Day 7

- Source 10 security/platform prospects.
- Prepare enterprise-readiness angle for prospects with customer-data, cloud, database, or code access.
- Update objections in the battlecard.

### Day 8

- Send approved messages.
- Move any serious replies to discovery.
- Use the discovery script to confirm scope and deadline.

### Day 9

- Follow up on Day 3 sends.
- Send scope/checkout follow-up to qualified interested prospects.
- Use MCP Launch Audit as the default recommendation.

### Day 10

- Review conversion:
  - sourced to qualified
  - qualified to sent
  - sent to reply
  - reply to interested
  - interested to paid
- Drop channels with no replies.
- Double down on the highest-reply persona.

### Day 11

- Source 10 more accounts from the highest-performing persona.
- Prepare 6 new drafts.
- Ask for warm intros from any friendly replies.

### Day 12

- Follow up on Day 6 sends.
- Send final follow-up to older quiet threads.
- Move no-response accounts toward Closed lost after final follow-up.

### Day 13

- Push for checkout on interested accounts with real urgency.
- Confirm package, delivery target, and safe intake.
- If payment happens, use the post-payment handoff immediately.

### Day 14

- Review all pipeline rows.
- Identify the top 3 objection patterns.
- Update outbound copy, battlecard, and qualification scorecard.
- Decide whether to raise pricing, narrow ICP, or source another 100 accounts.

## Reply Handling

| Reply Type | Next Move |
| --- | --- |
| "Send details" | Send one-page scope, sample report, and 4 qualification questions. |
| "How much?" | Recommend package based on scope; default to Launch Audit. |
| "We already have tools" | Use scanner-objection response from battlecard. |
| "Not now" | Ask whether MCP rollout has a future review date; set follow-up if concrete. |
| "Talk to security" | Ask for the AppSec/platform owner and offer the sample report. |
| No response | Follow up on business days 2, 6, and 12; then close lost. |

## Stop Conditions

Stop sending a given angle when:

- 30 targeted sends produce 0 replies
- replies show the buyer does not have MCP touching real systems
- the persona cannot buy a fixed-scope audit
- the channel risks spam or reputation damage
